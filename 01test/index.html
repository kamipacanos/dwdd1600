Digital Citizenship

What is Digital Citizenship?

Digital citizenship means using technology and the internet in a safe, responsible, respectful, and ethical way. It's basically how you should behave online, the same way being a good citizen is how you behave in real life.

What is Digital Law?

Digital law means the rules and laws that control how people use technology and the internet.It covers what is legal vs. illegal when you're online—like protecting privacy, preventing hacking, and respecting ownership of digital content.

What is AUP?

AUP stands for Acceptable Use Policy.It's a set of rules that explains what you are allowed and NOT allowed to do when using an organization's technology—like their Wi-Fi, computers, email, Canvas, or school/work accounts.

What is plagiarism?

Plagiarism is using someone else's work or ideas and presenting them as if they're your own—without giving credit.

What is Academic Dishonesty?

Academic dishonesty is any kind of cheating or unfair behavior in schoolwork—basically doing something that gives you credit for work that isn't honestly yours.

What are the rules for AI Generated Content?

AI-generated content rules usually depend on your teacher or school, but in general you should only use AI if it's allowed and you should never turn in AI work as if it's completely your own. AI is usually okay for helping you learn, like explaining concepts, brainstorming ideas, or checking grammar, but it's often not allowed to write full assignments, answer quizzes, or generate work you're supposed to create yourself. If your instructor requires it, you should also disclose or cite your AI use, and always double-check the information because you are responsible for anything you submit.
