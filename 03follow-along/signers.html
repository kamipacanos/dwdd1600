Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include

Thomas Jefferson (Virginia)
Main author of the Declaration of Independence and later the 3rd President of the United States.
John Hancock (Massachusetts)
President of the Continental Congress, famous for his large and bold signature.
Benjamin Franklin (Pennsylvania)
Inventor, diplomat, and Founding Father who served on the Committee of Five.
John Adams (Massachusetts)
Key leader in the movement for independence and later the 2nd President of the United States.
Samuel Adams (Massachusetts)
Major revolutionary leader and organizer of colonial resistance, especially in Boston.
Roger Sherman (Connecticut)
Member of the Committee of Five and an important figure in shaping the early U.S. government.
Robert Morris (Pennsylvania)
Powerful financier who helped fund the American Revolution, often called the “Financier of the Revolution.”
Charles Carroll of Carrollton (Maryland)
Wealthy and influential signer from Maryland and one of the most well-known Catholic Founding Fathers.

Each of these individuals played a significant role in the founding of the United States and is remembered for their courage in signing the Declaration, an act considered treasonous by the British Crown.
